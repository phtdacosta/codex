I'm **teo** — an engineer and builder working where software, hardware, and the questions that outlast both meet.

This codex is where I keep what I'm thinking through: notes on programming and engineering, forays into robotics and defense, the occasional experiment, and the philosophy that ties the rest together. I write to think, and I publish the parts worth keeping.

My bias is **e/acc**. I build in the open and I believe the way through is forward — better tools, made deliberately, put in the hands of people trying to do something worthy with them. Not acceleration for its own sake; acceleration ordered toward virtue. That tension — capability without wisdom — is most of what I end up writing about.

The name over the door is *gradatim per vitam* — step by step, through life. Progress compounds; so does patience. I'd rather build one true thing at a time than a hundred that don't hold.

You'll find me on [X](https://x.com/phteocos) and [GitHub](https://github.com/teocos). If something here earned its place in your day, you can [buy me a coffee](https://ko-fi.com/teocos) — it keeps the archive open and the lights on.
